My passion for music

A little bit of everything all of the time

Since I was a kid I've always shown interest in music. My parents(and especially my aunts) have always played music around me and my siblings. It's because of them that I have continued to show interest on listening to new artists from all genres of music.

The playlist

2025 is the second year on a row that I'm accomplishing a self-imposed challenged: an annual playlist, where each week I listen to a never-before-heard album.

It's a way to force myself to listen to new artists and not get stuck on the usual ones.
I don't know why I decided to start this, but I stick to it religiously.
The cover for the playlist is the most memorable photo I took during that year.

Favourite Artists

Over the years I have changed on who my favourite artist was. From Coldplay to Aretha Franklin, to Daft Punk, VVV[Trippin' you] and El Petit de Cal Eril.

I try to listen a little bit of everything from all styles. This year, I have been listening A LOT to PinkPantheress. I really don't know what kind of drug she puts into her songs but I'm addicted to it. I have no doubts she will be one of the most memorable artists of the 2020's.

Please now enjoy a little explainer on my current music taste and some of my favourite bands! (and why do I think they're cool!)


VVV [Trippin'you]

bakala, techno, post-punk

In 2021 I went to a Depresión Sonora concert and VVV were the opening act. I did not know nothing about them and I fell in love with their performance and their music. Even more than Depresión Sonora.

Since then, this band has been on my most listened artist on Spotify two years in a row! I love the depressive, angry and radical sound that they have going on.

I have many favourite tracks, but you should listen to Destrucción.

Daft Punk

electronic, french, 90's

For the few of you who might not who Daft Punk are, they are an electronic dance music french duo. They made a big impact in house music, electronic music during the 90's and 00's and their music is simply sublime.

They have 4 absurdly good albums, although if you ask me about my favourite of them it would be Discovery. I have Random Access Memories on vinyl, and it's definitely an experience.

Honestly you should just go and listen to them, you'll thank me afterwards.

P.S: You should listen to Touch.
